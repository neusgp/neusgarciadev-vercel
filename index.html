My journey

My story feels like a rollercoaster, with its twists and turns, ups and downs — but that’s life, and that’s how it’s meant to be, right? In 2017, I moved to Germany, and honestly, I haven’t stopped doing things since I arrived. Alongside my master’s studies at the Hochschule für Musik Karlsruhe, I worked as a piano and language teacher, and with the same energy I poured into that, I dove headfirst into the world of code — from user experience and interface design, to websites and functionality, all the way through to development. Getting a bit more technical now, here’s a list of some tools I enjoy using:

figma · notion · git · html · css · wordpress · javascript · typescript · react

Living abroad isn’t easy, and I’ve often missed my homeland, Catalonia. There’s a very important reason why I decided to stay in Karlsruhe: my friends. I believe in personal relationships — without them, it’s impossible to grow as an individual. I initially came here because of my love for music, but when I considered returning to Barcelona, I chose to stay for them and to continue growing professionally in Germany.

And now, what?

Right now, I’m looking for a job and I’m eager to apply all my knowledge and skills. That’s why I feel ready to take on any role: as an individual contributor, leading a team, teaching and mentoring, or working across disciplines to connect different teams or departments — always with the goal of improving the company with joy. Whatever it is, I bring passion and proactivity.
